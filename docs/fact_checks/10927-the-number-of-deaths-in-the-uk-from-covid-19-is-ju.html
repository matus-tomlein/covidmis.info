You’re more likely to drown than die from Covid-19.

This is not a valid comparison to make, and the figures it’s based on aren’t accurate.
"Germany and the United States are the two best in deaths per 100,000 people" for COVID-19.
May 11, 2020, factcheck.org
False
According to NHS England figures, the number of people whose death certificates show death from only coronavirus, i.e. no pre-existing condition, is 1,265.
Jun 26, 2020, fullfact.org
This was the correct number of Covid-19 deaths (but only among hospital patients) where no pre-existing condition was reported, up to 26 May. But this data isn’t the deaths “only” from coronavirus and doesn’t tell us what the underlying cause of death was. Covid-19 death was determined by a positive test result or where Covid-19 was mentioned on the death certificate.

False
The survival rate for Covid-19 is 98.54% in the UK.
May 19, 2020, fullfact.org
We don’t know what the survival rate is yet, as we don’t know how many people have had the disease. This figure comes from a claim about the US which is a slight over-estimate based on available figures.
Far more people have died of Covid-19 in the UK than in Japan
May 19, 2020, fullfact.org
This is correct. At the time this post appeared there had been 31,855 Covid-19 deaths reported in the UK, compared with 628 in Japan.
The ONS has registered 4,122 deaths in 2020 due to Covid-19.
May 12, 2020, fullfact.org
This was broadly correct for the first 14 weeks of the year, including a few days in April and in December 2019.

True
About 500 lions have been released on the streets of Russia to stop people going out during the coronavirus pandemic.
Mar 24, 2020, fullfact.org
This is not true. The Russian government has yet to announce a widespread lockdown of any kind in Russia. The image of the lion is from South Africa in 2016.
The total number of deaths from Covid-19 in the UK is now at 233, the same number of deaths reported in Italy about two weeks ago.
Mar 24, 2020, fullfact.org
Correct. On 21 March, Public Health England reported a total of 233 deaths in the UK from Covid-19. However, not enough is known to say whether the number of deaths will grow at the same rate in the UK as in Italy.
The total number of deaths from Covid-19 in Italy rose from 233 to 4,032 over about two weeks.
Mar 24, 2020, fullfact.org
Correct. As reported by the Italian government, the number of deaths from Covid-19 rose from 233 on 7 March to 4,032 on 20 March.
A group of 43 European countries and territories have far more people than the UK, but fewer Covid-19 deaths.
Jun 29, 2020, fullfact.org
This is correct. Although the list excludes other European countries with high death tolls, such as France, Italy, Spain and Belgium.
The UK is doing more Covid-19 tests than virtually any country in Europe.
Jun 02, 2020, fullfact.org
The UK appears to be testing fewer people than in Russia. But there isn’t enough data on this to determine for sure where the UK places, and the UK’s data isn’t particularly comparable with other countries.
The deaths of 181 NHS workers have involved Covid-19.
May 22, 2020, fullfact.org
This figure is correct for England only.
96.3% of the Italy’s Covid-19 deaths were actually caused by other diseases.
May 22, 2020, fullfact.org
This is incorrect. Official data shows that 96.3% of Covid-19 patients had other health conditions when they died.
More than a third of Covid-19 deaths had diabetes.
May 22, 2020, fullfact.org
This is incorrect. NHS data shows that of Covid-19 patients who died in hospital in England just over a quarter had diabetes.
An estimated 0.5% of people arriving into the UK have Covid-19.
May 21, 2020, fullfact.org
These were remarks by the Home Office’s Chief Scientific Advisor, which were later clarified to be wrong. 0.5% is an estimate for the proportion of reported Covid-19 cases in the UK believed to have been “imported” from abroad as of 23 March.
Covid-19 deaths in care homes are rising.
May 14, 2020, fullfact.org
This was true at the time, by one measure. By another measure it was probably not true. Now it seems clearer that deaths in care homes from suspected Covid-19 are falling.
It took 51 days for the UK to go from announcing its first Covid-19 death to 20,319.
Apr 27, 2020, fullfact.org
Correct. The first patient who died having tested positive for Covid-19 was announced on 5 March. The total deaths was 20,319 on 24 April.
19 million people in the UK have already been infected with the new coronavirus.
May 27, 2020, fullfact.org
This is according to a new piece of statistical research, but this research has been challenged by other scientists and is highly uncertain. Other early estimates have estimated the prevalence to be lower, but it’s impossible to tell how accurate these will turn out to be.
The official UK death toll from the coronavirus pandemic now equals the death toll from the Blitz.
Jun 24, 2020, fullfact.org
At the time this claim was made, the government’s “official” published count of Covid-19 deaths was slightly under the number of deaths during the Blitz, although the numbers are very similar. The true number of deaths related to Covid-19 is likely to be higher than the toll during the Blitz.
